About

I am a PhD candidate working on theoretical and computational astrophysics, with a focus on axion electrodynamics, compact objects, binary neutron-star systems, gravitational waves, and dark-matter searches with multimessenger signals.

Research

My research explores how compact astrophysical systems can be used as laboratories for beyond-standard-model physics. In particular, I study axion-like particles coupled to electromagnetic fields in relativistic and strongly magnetized environments.

  • Axion electrodynamics in curved spacetime
  • Dark matter around neutron stars and compact binaries
  • Axion-photon conversion in magnetized neutron-star systems
  • Gravitational-wave probes of hidden-sector physics
  • Numerical tools for relativistic field evolution
