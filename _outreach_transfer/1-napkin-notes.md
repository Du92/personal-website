# Napkin Notes

Napkin Notes is a science communication blog–magazine created with friends as
a space for clear ideas, everyday science, quick estimates, and curiosity-driven
questions.

The project is built around a simple premise: many interesting scientific
questions can begin with a rough calculation, an intuitive argument, or a
Fermi-style estimate written on a napkin. Instead of aiming for excessive
formalism, Napkin Notes uses creativity, humor, and accessible reasoning to
explore everyday phenomena, mathematical curiosities, and scientific questions
that invite readers to think.

## Project focus

- Science communication for a broad audience.
- Everyday physics and mathematics.
- Fermi-style estimates and back-of-the-envelope reasoning.
- Short, accessible articles with a light and playful tone.
- Curiosity-driven explanations of ordinary and unexpected phenomena.

## My role

I co-developed the project and contribute to its editorial and technical
direction, including concept design, website development, article planning, and
science communication strategy.
