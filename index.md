<section class="section">
  <h2>Research profile</h2>

  <p>
    I am a researcher in theoretical and computational
    astrophysics at the University of Salamanca. My work focuses on compact
    objects as laboratories for dark-matter and astroparticle physics, with
    particular emphasis on neutron stars, gravitational waves, axion-like
    particles, and electromagnetic signatures of hidden-sector interactions.
  </p>

  <p>
    My research trajectory connects the study of boson stars and gravastars
    with current work on dark matter in neutron stars, gravitational-wave
    observables, and ALP–photon couplings in strongly magnetized compact
    systems.
  </p>

  <ul class="clean-list">
    <li>Axion and ALP phenomenology in compact-object environments</li>
    <li>Dark-matter interactions in neutron stars and binary neutron-star systems</li>
    <li>Gravitational-wave probes of dark sectors</li>
    <li>Axion-photon conversion in magnetized astrophysical plasmas</li>
    <li>Numerical modeling, equation-of-state studies, and computational pipelines</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <h2>Selected highlights</h2>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>Compact objects as dark-sector laboratories</h3>
    <p>
      My current research investigates how neutron stars and compact binaries
      can encode signatures of dark matter, axion-like particles, and hidden
      sectors in gravitational-wave and electromagnetic observables.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>Publications and collaborations</h3>
    <p>
      I have authored and co-authored several publications in relativistic
      astrophysics and astroparticle physics, including first-author works in
      Physical Review D and Physics Letters B, and I contributed to the
      Einstein Telescope Collaboration science case.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>Scientific software</h3>
    <p>
      I develop numerical and data-driven tools for astrophysical modeling,
      including Python-based pipelines for axion electrodynamics, compact
      objects, dark-matter searches, and simulation post-processing.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>
